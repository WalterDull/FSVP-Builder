2. Suppliers & Products

Add one entry for each foreign supplier and food product combination covered by this FSVP plan. Most importers need at least one; add as many as apply — this plan, and its $699 unlock, covers all of them.

Not sure what counts as a "hazard requiring a control"? Work through this for each entry below:

  1. Q1: Is this a known or reasonably foreseeable hazard for this food? If no, it's not a hazard for FSVP purposes — skip it.
  2. Q2: Is the food "covered produce" and this a biological hazard? If yes, it's automatically deemed to require a control.
  3. Q3: Weighing severity and probability absent controls, would a knowledgeable person establish a control? If yes, it's a "Hazard Requiring a Control." If you're unsure, err toward yes and document your reasoning.
  4. Q4: Will it be controlled by your supplier (or something upstream of them), or by your customer/someone downstream? This determines whether you need full verification or the lighter written-assurance pathway.

Common trap: a processing step only "controls" a hazard if the step's own parameters (time, temperature, etc.) achieve it. If control actually depends on an SOP (sanitation, allergen changeover) being followed around the step, the SOP is the real control — not the step itself.
