What Is an FSVP?

A Foreign Supplier Verification Program (FSVP) is a food safety compliance program required under the FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA), codified at 21 CFR Part 1, Subpart L. U.S. importers of FDA-regulated food must verify that their foreign suppliers produce food that meets the same public health protections as domestic producers. An FSVP must be developed by a qualified individual and kept up to date as suppliers, products, or risks change.

Who Needs an FSVP?

Most importers of human and animal food regulated by the FDA need an FSVP for each food and foreign supplier combination, with some exemptions (for example, certain juice and seafood HACCP products already covered by other FDA programs, and food imported for personal use). This tool helps you organize the required elements of your program; it does not replace a qualified individual's review or legal advice specific to your situation.
